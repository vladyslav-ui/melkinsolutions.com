Systems Engineering

The HAGES Architecture

A five-layer operational stack combining GPS-denied SLAM perception, aerial agility, persistent ground mobility, autonomous mid-mission recharging, and decision-ready data intelligence.

Integrated Systems Stack

HAGES is not a standalone drone or a standalone rover. It is a fully integrated, multi-modal robotic network designed specifically to shatter the endurance wall of underground inspection.

Layer 01: Perception & Autonomy

Emesent Hovermap ST-X

The core intelligence of the system. Operating in GPS-denied environments, the Hovermap ST-X utilizes advanced SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) to actively build a 3D map of its surroundings, enabling omnidirectional collision avoidance and fully autonomous path planning underground.

Sensing Capacity 32 Lasers, 3 Returns
Max Range 0.5m to 300m
Data Acquisition Speed 1.92M points/sec
Accuracy (Underground) ±10mm
Layer 02: Aerial Agility

Custom UAV Propulsion

The agility asset. Built around a robust flight controller and ALPHA 40A ESC stack, the quadcopter frame is specifically optimized for high-efficiency flight in confined spaces characterized by dusty, turbulent underground airflows.

Motor Architecture T-Motor MN5008 KV340
Propeller Pitch P15×5 Carbon Fiber
Flight System Cube Orange+
Layer 03: Ground Persistence

Overlander-T Crawler Base

The persistent backbone of the operation. This rugged, tracked mobile base acts as the transport vector, carrying the UAV deep into the hazard zone. It serves as a mobile launchpad and the primary energy reserve for the entire hybrid system.

Traction System 48mm Badlands Tank-Tracks
Payload Capacity 25 lbs (11.3 kg)
Energy Reserve 24V 30Ah LiFePO4 Dual-Pack
Geometry Flat skid-plate underbelly
Layer 04: Data Intelligence

Emesent Aura Processing

The intelligence engine. While onboard processing can generate rapid, locally correct point clouds in the field, Emesent Aura utilizes rigorous global optimization algorithms to eliminate drift and produce high-resolution, survey-grade deliverables across multi-kilometer trajectories.

Onboard Fast-Processing ~80s (15 min mission)
Aura Global Optimization ~1,200s (15 min mission)
Cloud-to-Cloud Accuracy 6.7mm at 1-sigma

The Mid-Mission Recharge Loop

The defining breakthrough of HAGES 1.0 is the elimination of the UAV endurance wall. By integrating HEISHA’s industrial charging technology, the UAV can land, secure itself, recharge, and relaunch entirely autonomously while deep underground.

Rover Integration

HEISHA DPad C500 / D60

5-in-1 Precision PCB

A highly integrated internal control board replaces complex wiring looms, drastically improving the stability of the charging sequence and minimizing points of failure in high-vibration environments.

Infrared Centering & Limiter

Utilizes positive and negative limiters paired with an infrared sensor and sun-blocking shade to ensure the drone centers perfectly on the pad upon landing, regardless of lighting conditions.

Mechanical Locking Bars

Once the UAV has landed, specialized clamping bars deploy to physically lock the drone to the rover. This prevents the UAV from shifting or falling while the rover navigates rough, uneven terrain to the next deployment zone.

UAV Integration

D60 Smart Landing Gear

High-Current Gold Electrodes

Fitted with gold-plated dual electrodes capable of transferring up to 10A of current. This allows the UAV to rapidly pull power from the rover’s 24V 30Ah LiFePO4 energy reserve, slashing downtime between sorties.

Ultra-Lightweight Profile

Weighing only 38 grams, the D60 landing gear integrates seamlessly onto the custom quadcopter frame without impacting the UAV's payload capacity or flight efficiency.

Industrial Fatigue Rating

Engineered for continuous, repetitive enterprise use, the mechanical and electrical contact points are rated and tested for ≥ 5,000 continuous landing and charging fatigue cycles.
