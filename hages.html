HAGES 1.0

The Hybrid Aerial & Ground Explorer System. An integrated two-platform solution engineered specifically for high-consequence underground and confined space inspection.

Hardware Architecture

GROUND_SEGMENT

The Rover Platform

A ruggedized, tracked mobile base designed to transport the aerial asset deep into the hazard zone and act as an active staging ground.

Foxtech Crawler Tracks

Plug-and-play crawler track system engineered for rough, uneven terrain and rocky surfaces. It provides the core mobility for the system without requiring complex custom integrations.

Rover Energy System

Powered by a 24V 30Ah LiFePO4 dual-battery pack, coupled with a DC-AC inverter. This high-capacity system drives ground operations while providing sufficient reserve power to recharge the UAV mid-mission.

HEISHA DPad C500

Integrated onto the top of the rover, this platform acts as the landing and charging pad. It features black channels designed to physically align and lock the drone securely in place while the rover is in motion.

AERIAL_SEGMENT

The UAV Platform

A highly specialized, autonomous quadcopter designed to launch from the rover, map complex 3D voids, and autonomously dock for recharging.

Custom Quadcopter Frame

Built around a Cube Orange+ Flight Controller and an ALPHA 40A ESC stack. It utilizes T-Motors MN5008 KV340 outrunners paired with P15x5 carbon fiber props—a pitch optimized for maximum flight efficiency at low RPMs in dusty, turbulent underground airflows.

Emesent Hovermap ST-X

The primary sensing payload. Provides GPS-denied SLAM navigation and 3D LiDAR mapping. Features a 32-channel scanner with a 300m range, capturing 1.92 million points per second with ±10mm accuracy underground. Enables fully autonomous flight without GPS infrastructure.

HEISHA D60 Landing Gear

An ultra-lightweight (38g) wireless recharging landing gear system. It allows the drone to automatically align with the DPad C500 on the rover, executing autonomous landing, charging, and relaunching sequences with a lifespan of ≥5,000 fatigue cycles.

SYSTEM_INTEGRATION

The Mid-Mission Recharge Loop

The core architectural claim of HAGES 1.0 is the successful integration of the rover and UAV systems. By enabling the drone to land on the crawler, securely lock into place, wirelessly recharge from the rover's energy system, and autonomously relaunch, HAGES shatters the endurance limitations of traditional single-mode robots.
